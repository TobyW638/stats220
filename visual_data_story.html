Plot 1

Full closed shoes vs open shoes

In this first plot it is looking at the amount of shoes that are enclosed or not. This was done by observing if the shoes were fully covered or not. When doing this I did expect to see more enclosed shoes as it is more common which the graph shows. Taking my second set of data, I did seem to notice that there were fewer open shoes, such as sandals and flip flops, I think this was because there was colder wetter weather as we more further away from summer. Looking at the data the number of observations of enclosed shoes is much high than the observations for non enclosed shoes.

Plot 2

Brands that I thought had the best shoes

Looking at plot 2 it is a box plot graph that shows the different shoe brands that were seen in my observations vs the rating that I would give them of how good I think they look on a scale of 1-5. I have ordered them top to bottom in lowest to highest mean rating, with Adidas being the highest and also having the most 5/5 ratings on appearance.

Plot 3

Most popular brands

The 3rd plot shows the brand name and how many times it was seen by university students. It show that Nike is the most common brand followed by Adidas when it comes to what is being wore by university students. This shows that Nike definitely have control over the shoe industry.
